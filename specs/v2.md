# Reflo — Version 2 Spec (The Audit Log)

v1 threw everything away on purpose. Every quiz, every miss, every narration —
shown, then gone. "Shown, then gone" was a stance, not a shortcut: it kept the
app a mirror you walk up to, not a thing that keeps a file on you.

v2 makes one deliberate departure from that stance. The app stops throwing the
quiz away. It keeps an honest, complete record of what happened — every quiz
photographed whole and written down. Nothing scored, nothing personalized, nothing
fed back into the live quiz flow. Just *kept* — and, when you choose, *re-read*.

*This is the foundation, not the feature. Everything we might one day do with the
past — drill the ideas you slipped on, catch a returning idea in a later chapter,
notice the kind of trap you keep falling for — all of it sits downstream of the
record existing. You can't pull a slip forward until the slip is written down.
v2 writes it down. That's the whole job.*

---

## What changes (the entire diff from v1)

Three things:

1. **The quiz call returns more.** Each option now comes back tagged with a
   **trap type** drawn from a fixed list — not just the correct mark, but *what
   kind of misunderstanding* each wrong option is built to exploit. Correct
   options carry `"trap_type": null`.
2. **Every quiz sitting gets photographed — durably and resumably.** A new quiz
   receives a UUID before generation. After generation, one complete **draft**
   JSON is created exclusively and durably before question 1 becomes visible.
   Each legal transition atomically replaces that draft. You can force-quit and
   resume exactly where you left off.
3. **Completed attempts are immutable.** When the narration reply succeeds, the
   draft moves to finalization, an immutable **completed attempt** is written,
   and the matching draft is removed. Past attempts can be opened and re-read
   whole. They are never updated or deleted.

The four brain trips keep their shapes. The live quiz screens keep their flow.
Narration reply still receives only chapter text, not quiz answers. v2 is a
recording layer plus one extra field on one existing call — plus resume and
read-back for the record itself.

A five-year-old version: *the app used to forget every quiz the moment it ended.
Now it keeps a little book of everything that happened, so it never forgets —
and you can pick up a half-written page or read an old one anytime.*

---

## The one field that has to be caught now or never

Most of the photograph is easy. The chapter text, the question wording, the
options, your tap, your narration, the reply — all of that is sitting around at
the moment of the quiz, easy to grab.

The **trap type** is the exception. When the brain makes a quiz, it knows each
distractor is a specific kind of tempting wrongness — but v1 never *kept* that
reasoning; the user only ever saw four bare options, and the reasoning
evaporated. For the log to record the trap behind each option, the **quiz call
has to hand the trap type back with each option**, and the app has to write it
down *right then*. Reconstructed later, it's gone — the question was spun once and
would be spun differently next time.

This is the single seam where v2 reaches back and touches v1's design: the quiz
trip must now *emit* the trap, not use it silently.

### Wire format

Every option in the quiz JSON contains `"trap_type"`:

- Correct option: `"trap_type": null`
- Each distractor: one required closed-list string

The legacy `depth` field is temporary migration input only and is removed from
the v2 prompt contract.

---

## Why type the traps (and why correct options have none)

The trap type isn't decoration on the record. It's the thing that lets the log
eventually learn a *shape* instead of telling one-off stories. Counted across the
whole log, trap types answer a question no single attempt can: *what kind of trap
does this reader keep falling for?*

Crucially, the type is captured on **every distractor**, and correct options
carry **no trap type**. The signal you actually care about is a rate, and a rate
needs both numbers:

- Miss the type only on a miss, and you can count your misses — but you've thrown
  away the denominator. You'd know you missed three ontological traps without
  knowing you *faced* thirty.
- Tag every distractor, and the reading-back can compute the honest thing: *of the
  traps of type T you faced, you fell for this many.*

Sailing past a chapter full of one kind of trap says something. Hitting the one
trap in a chapter that only had one says something louder. Only the full tagging
catches the difference.

---

## The trap-type list

The brain picks each trap's type from a **fixed, closed list** — not free text.
A closed set is what keeps the data countable; an open vocabulary would fill the
log with near-synonyms nobody can tally.

The list is a **living document, tuned against real chapters** — the same way the
quiz prompt is. You start with a principled set, watch it meet actual misses, and
adjust. A box that never matches a real miss is dead weight; a real reason with no
box gets silently mislabeled forever. The list earns its shape from real books,
not from the whiteboard.

The principled starting spine:

- **`false_belief`** — a plain wrong fact, swappable for the right one.
- **`flawed_mental_model`** — the right pieces, wired together wrong.
- **`ontological_miscategorization`** — the thing filed in the wrong *kind* of box
  (a flow treated as a stock, a process treated as a thing).
- **`overturned_common_sense_belief`** — the surface-plausible idea the chapter was
  written to defeat. Often the most tempting distractor of all.
- **`unclassified`** — the escape hatch when the brain genuinely can't fit a trap
  cleanly. Both a safety valve and a tuning signal.

Two honest caveats carried forward, not solved here:
- Typing is a judgment, and a single distractor can genuinely be two types at
  once. The patterns are suggestive, not clinical.
- A closed set doesn't make misfits disappear — it hides them, by forcing the
  least-wrong label. So the list staying genuinely exhaustive-enough is what keeps
  the data from getting mushier than it looks.

## Two tiers of storage

v2 uses a **two-tier local record**:

### Mutable drafts (resumable)

- One JSON file per unfinished quiz sitting.
- Created exclusively after quiz generation, before question 1 is shown.
- Updated atomically on every legal transition with revision checking.
- Multiple unfinished drafts per chapter are allowed.
- Users may explicitly discard a draft after confirmation. Drafts never
  auto-expire.
- Stored under Application Support (not Documents), because file sharing exposes
  Documents to external edits.

### Immutable completed attempts (append-only)

- One JSON file per finished quiz sitting.
- Created when the narration reply succeeds and finalization runs.
- Never updated or deleted. No update or delete API.
- The matching draft is removed after the attempt file is written and validated.
- Also under Application Support.

---

## Durable state machine

Each draft carries `schemaVersion`, a stable UUID, `revision`, store-owned
`createdAt`/`updatedAt`, a frozen chapter snapshot, exact shuffled
questions/options, per-question answer/generated-content state, final transcript,
and one of these stages:

1. **`answering(questionIndex)`** — current question is unanswered.
2. **`mendingPending(questionIndex, requestID)`** — wrong selection is durable;
   mending may now be requested.
3. **`mendingReview(questionIndex)`** — displayed mending outcome is durable;
   second example is absent, succeeded, or failed.
4. **`secondExamplePending(questionIndex, requestID)`** — request intent is
   durable; the call may now start.
5. **`narrating`** — every question has a durable answer and any displayed
   remediation content.
6. **`replyPending(requestID)`** — the final nonempty transcript is durable;
   narration feedback may now be requested.
7. **`replyFailed`** — transcript and failure are durable; retry creates a new
   request ID and returns to `replyPending`.
8. **`finalizationPending`** — reply text and completion timestamp are durable;
   no brain call is allowed. Relaunch from this stage finalizes without calling
   the brain again.

### Transition rules

- A correct selection atomically stores the answer and advances to the next
  `answering` stage or `narrating`; UI advancement waits for the save.
- A wrong selection atomically stores the answer and `mendingPending` before the
  mending call starts.
- Mending/second-example results, including exact fallback/error text shown, are
  saved before display.
- Continue from mending atomically advances to the next question or narration.
- Stopping narration recording performs a revision-checked `narrating → narrating`
  transition that replaces the durable transcript. The UI marks stop complete only
  after the save. A failed save retains the previous durable transcript and offers
  retry. **Force-quitting during active recording may lose the current partial
  transcription.**
- Continue from narration saves the final transcript and `replyPending` before the
  reply call starts.
- Reply success saves `finalizationPending` before finalization. Finalization
  immediately creates the immutable attempt and removes the matching draft. Done
  only navigates away.
- Any save failure leaves the prior visible/durable stage unchanged.

## What gets photographed (one attempt = one frozen snapshot)

An attempt is stored **whole and self-contained** — everything needed to re-read
it lives inside the entry, because a regenerated quiz would never come back the
same. The snapshot holds:

| Captured | Notes |
|---|---|
| Book + chapter | Enough to anchor which reading this was |
| Chapter text | Frozen at quiz time |
| Each question, exactly as worded | Frozen — this wording exists nowhere else |
| Every option, exactly as worded | All of them, not just the right one |
| Correct mark | Which option was right |
| **Trap type per distractor** | From the fixed list; `null` on correct; caught at quiz time or lost |
| Your tap | Which option you chose |
| Mending paragraph | If you missed and one was shown |
| Second example | If you asked for one |
| Narration (text) | Your transcribed spoken summary |
| Narration reply | The warm-then-honest words, no score |
| Timestamps | Created and completed |

---

## How the log is shaped (grain)

**v2 stores at session grain** — one photographed attempt per sitting — because
re-reading a whole past quiz is the feature you actually asked for. Pattern reads
across trap types require scanning session entries; a question-grain index is a
deliberate later step.

## What lives where

| Concern | Home | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Library of EPUBs | Documents/Books | Still persists (unchanged) |
| **Audit log (drafts + attempts)** | **Application Support/QuizAudit** | **New. Persists alongside the library.** |
| Making quiz questions / traps | Rented brain | **Now also returns trap type per option** |
| Mending paragraph / second example / narration reply | Rented brain | Unchanged |

No backend, no accounts, no sync.

## Resume and read-back UX

v2 adds navigation for the record without changing live quiz behavior:

- **Global Quizzes screen** — lists all drafts and completed attempts.
- **Chapter-level actions** — Start New (always fresh UUID), Resume Latest (by
  store ordering), and access to all chapter drafts/attempts.
- **Draft discard** — explicit, confirmed; never affects completed attempts.
- **Completed detail** — factual, read-only replay of the exact captured content.
  No score, aggregate trap analysis, verdict, editing, or deletion.

Restoring and displaying one selected quiz or attempt is allowed. Cross-attempt
analysis or influence on the live quiz flow remains out.

---

## Explicitly OUT of v2 (deliberately deferred)

The data accumulates **silently in the live flow**. v2 records; it does not read
back into quiz behavior. Everything that *uses* the log for active behavior is
parked:

- **Revision quiz.** Gathering slips into a dedicated review session.
- **Returning-idea callbacks.** Weaving old missed ideas into later quizzes.
- **Pattern verdicts.** Showing trap tendencies to the user.
- **Personalization by trap-type performance.** Still deferred.
- **Feeding quiz answers into narration reply.** v2 keeps `reply(narration:chapterText:)`
  unchanged on purpose.

**A tension worth naming, not resolving.** v1's narration-reply call deliberately
carries *the chapter, not your quiz answers*. v2 photographs those answers into the
log, but still withholds them from the narration call. Feeding quiz results into
the reply is a real v3 move. v2 keeps the call exactly as it was.

The rule of thumb: **v2 writes the record and stops influencing the live flow.**
Read-back of past attempts is allowed; using the log to change how the next quiz
behaves is not.

---

## Honest hard parts

- **Trap types are caught at quiz time or never.**
- **The list will feel wrong before it feels right.** Expect retuning against real
  chapters.
- **One label flattens a two-natured trap.** Live with it.
- **Completed attempts only grow.** Drafts are mutable until completion or discard.
- **Pattern reads are slow until you index.** Session-grain storage answers trap
  questions by scanning.
- **Partial transcript during recording is not durable.** Only stopped or
  continued transcripts are persisted.

---

## Definition of done for v2

On your own iPhone, on top of the working shell:

1. Run a quiz and have each distractor come back tagged with a trap type from the
   fixed list; correct options have no trap type.
2. Confirm the draft is created durably before question 1 appears; force-quit and
   resume from every stage.
3. Complete a full quiz event — taps, any miss, narration (after stop/continue),
   reply — and see an immutable completed attempt written.
4. Confirm drafts and attempts persist across app launches under Application
   Support.
5. Open a past attempt and re-read it whole — exact questions, every option, trap
   behind each distractor, what you tapped, what was said back.
6. Start multiple quizzes for the same chapter; resume and discard specific drafts
   by exact ID.
7. Confirm nothing about the log is surfaced into the live quiz flow — no
   callbacks, no review, no verdicts, no trap-pattern reads during quizzing. The
   global/chapter history UI is read-only replay only.

When all seven hold, v2 is done.
